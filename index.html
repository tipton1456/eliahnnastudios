3. Information we collect

3.1 Information from Pinterest (when you connect an account)

If you authorize our application via Pinterest OAuth, we may receive and process:

  • Pinterest user account identifiers and basic profile information (for example, username or account name)
  • Board names and board identifiers that you allow the app to access
  • Pin metadata related to Pins created or managed through the app (titles, descriptions, links, media references, creation timestamps, and Pin IDs)
  • OAuth access tokens and refresh tokens needed to call the Pinterest API on your behalf
  • Permission scopes you approve (for example, reading boards and creating Pins)

3.2 Information you provide directly

  • Configuration values you supply (for example, destination board IDs, product/listing URLs, and content preferences)
  • Communications you send us (email support or privacy requests)

3.3 Technical and operational information

  • Application logs related to publishing success/failure (timestamps, error codes, topic or template identifiers)
  • Device/browser information only if you visit this website (standard server or host logs such as IP address, user agent, and request time)

3.4 Information we do not intentionally collect

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal information (such as government IDs, precise health data, or financial account numbers) through the Pinterest application. We do not sell personal information.

4. How we use information

We use the information described above to:

  • Authenticate with Pinterest and maintain authorized API access
  • Create, schedule, and publish Pins to boards you select
  • Attach destination links (for example, an Etsy product listing) and promotional media
  • Avoid duplicate posts, track publishing history, and improve reliability of the automation
  • Provide support, fix errors, and maintain security of the application
  • Comply with law and enforce our terms

We process this information based on your consent (OAuth authorization), our legitimate interests in operating our business tools, and/or performance of a service you request.

5. How we share information

We may share information only as follows:

  • Pinterest: Content and API requests are sent to Pinterest to create and manage Pins according to the permissions you grant. Pinterest processes data under its own policies: Pinterest Privacy Policy.
  • Service providers: Hosting, logging, or infrastructure providers that process data solely to help us run the service, under appropriate confidentiality obligations.
  • Legal requirements: If required by law, regulation, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, and security.
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, with notice where required.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

6. Data storage and security

  • OAuth tokens and configuration are stored in secured local or private environment storage used to operate the app, not in public repositories.
  • We use reasonable administrative and technical safeguards appropriate to the nature of the data (access controls, least-privilege credentials, and secret handling practices).
  • No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data retention

We retain information only as long as needed for the purposes above:

  • OAuth tokens: until you disconnect the app, tokens expire/revoke, or you request deletion
  • Publishing logs: typically retained for operational history and troubleshooting, then deleted or anonymized when no longer needed
